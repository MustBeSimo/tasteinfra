07 — The Gatekeeper Model

Your stack is a funnel, not a checklist.

Most companies treat evaluation like a post-ship audit. Winners build rejection gates before anything reaches production.

Generic Taste Infrastructure Template (Gatekeeper Model)

INPUT: AI-generated candidate (Draft, Image, Code, Response)
↓ (1,000 candidates generated)

GATE 1: SAFETY & COMPLIANCE
├── Check: PII detection, brand safety, regulatory alignment
├── Tool: Content moderation layer + custom regex/ML filters
├── Reject if: Prohibited content or legal risk detected
└── Survival: 99.8%
↓

GATE 2: TECHNICAL CORRECTNESS
├── Check: Logical consistency, factual accuracy, syntax
├── Tool: Golden set comparison + automated verification
├── Reject if: Hallucinations or technical artifacts present
└── Survival: 94%
↓

GATE 3: BRAND FIT & STYLE
├── Check: Tone of voice, visual language, style guide alignment
├── Tool: LLM-as-a-Judge + brand-specific embeddings
├── Reject if: Off-brand tone or stylistic deviation
└── Survival: 87%
↓

GATE 4: EXCELLENCE (TASTE)
├── Check: High-end quality, creative spark, human-like nuance
├── Tool: Reward model ranker (trained on expert judgments) + human review for edge cases (top 5% uncertainty)
├── Reject if: "Average" or uninspired output
└── Survival: 78%
↓

SHIP: 64% final pass rate
Outcome: Quality becomes the edge. Near-zero critical leaks.

07b — Honest Limitations

The Cost of Control

Rigorous evaluation is not free. It is a strategic choice with real trade-offs.

Latency

Better judges "think" longer. Adding a robust LLM-as-a-Judge layer can add 500ms - 2s to your response time.

Verdict: Unfit for real-time voice; essential for async email/reports.

False Negatives

Strict gates kill serendipity. An overly conservative "Brand Gate" will reject creative outliers that don't fit the training distribution.

Verdict: You must tune "Temperature" for the Gate, not just the Generator.

Maintenance

Your "Golden Set" rots faster than your code. As models change (weekly), your benchmarks become obsolete.

Verdict: You need a dedicated "Eval Ops" owner. It's not a set-and-forget script.

The Commoditization Question

Won't evaluation get cheaper too? Yes, for basic checks. Syntax validation, factual retrieval, and policy compliance are commoditizing fast.

But: Consequence-weighted judgment (brand risk, legal exposure, domain expertise) remains expensive because it's context-specific and liability-bearing.
